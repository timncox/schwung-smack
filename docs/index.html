01 · What it is

Four moves, one loop

Smack is a performance looper-mangler in the family of Sugar Bytes Looperator and dblue Glitch, built for hands-on hardware use on the Move. It records incoming audio — mic, line, USB-C, or whatever is upstream in the Signal Chain — into a continuously running 70-second ring buffer.

Capture is retroactive: press it and the last 1 step to 16 bars you already played become the loop, aligned to the clock grid. No punch-in anxiety. Prefer to record forward? Arm does that.

The loop is sliced on the grid, then a seeded roll hands out effects and slice-order swaps at the densities you set. Seed 1234 always produces pattern 1234 — the Seed knob literally browses patterns by number, and Re-Roll deals a fresh hand.

A/B punches between the untouched loop and the pattern, quantized so it always lands musically. Every slice can be inspected, muted, pinned to a chosen effect, or tweaked — from the pads or from a browser.

STEP 1

Capture

Grab the last N bars retroactively, or arm and record forward. Clock-synced, half-step aligned.

Grid slicing at ½-step to 4-step resolution. Up to 16 slices per page on the step buttons.

STEP 3

Seeded randomness assigns one of 22 effects per slice and shuffles play order, at your densities.

STEP 4

LEN vs RES — the one people mix up. Len is how much audio the loop holds (1 step to 16 bars). Res is how finely that loop is diced for the pattern (one slice every ½, 1, 2, or 4 steps). Long loop + fine resolution = many small slices = busy glitch; short loop + coarse resolution = a few big chunks. Changing Res re-slices the same audio — it never changes what was captured.
05 · Workflow

Capture → roll → punch

Capture (retro). Play something. Press Capture (pad or the hardware Capture button). The last Loop-Length of audio becomes the loop instantly — Smack was already listening. The ring keeps recording while the loop plays, so pressing Capture again grabs whatever you played since.

Arm (forward). Press Arm; recording starts on the grid and loops automatically when Loop-Length is reached. The pad burns bright red while armed/recording.

A/B. Tap to flip between the clean loop (A) and the pattern (B). On oversmack, hold the pad for a momentary punch — it flips while held and snaps back on release. Flips are quantized per the QNT knob: Instant, next Slice, or next Loop boundary.

Re-Roll. Deals a new pattern from the current seed stream. Same knobs, new dice. The SEED knob browses patterns by number instead — seed 1234 is always the same pattern for the same loop settings, so you can write your favorites down.

Steps. The 16 step buttons show the pattern — one color per effect family, white playhead chasing through. In the chain UI a step press mutes that slice's effect (press again to restore the roll). On oversmack a step press selects the slice for editing with the palette. Loops with more than 16 slices page: jog wheel (oversmack) or pad 72 (chain).

Clear. Drops the loop and returns to idle. The ring keeps recording; nothing else is touched.

Deterministic by design. The pattern — effects, their parameters, and slice order — is locked to the seed. It repeats bit-identically every loop pass until you re-roll, change the seed, or change a density. Randomness you can rehearse.

07 · Pinning & punch

Bend the dice

On oversmack, the upper three pad rows are the effect palette, grouped by family and lit in family colors. The arrangement is yours to change — Arrange pads in the web editor swaps any two pads, the hardware follows instantly, and the layout persists in presets. With a slice selected on the steps:

Tap a palette pad → assign that effect to the slice.
Re-tap the same effect → lock it exactly as it currently sounds (keeps the rolled parameter).
Hold ≥ ½ s → pin it. Pins survive Re-Roll — build a skeleton of moments you love, then re-roll everything around them.
Unlock (top-right pad) → hand the slice back to the dice.

Pinning never disturbs its neighbors: unlocked slices roll identically with or without pins, so a pattern you save today restores exactly tomorrow.

Global punch. Hold a palette pad with no slice selected and that one effect takes over the whole loop, in original order, for as long as you hold — pad pressure bends its parameter while you lean in. Release, and the pattern comes back untouched. Instant fills: hold Buzz, lean, release.

Mute vs clean. In the chain UI, a step press mutes a slice's effect (temporary, roll preserved). On oversmack, assigning Clean from the palette does the equivalent — and Unlock restores the roll.

08 · Web editor

The loop, in a browser

smack-in and oversmack ship with a full browser editor, served by schwung-manager from the Move itself — open move.local:7700 → Remote UI. smack-in appears in its slot; oversmack under the Tool tab. Live sync both ways: pad moves show up in the browser, browser edits hit the pads instantly.

Per-slice tweaking. Click any slice, pick any effect, and set its parameter from a menu of musical options (the same lists as above) — finer control than the pad palette, same engine.

Effect presets. Save a favorite effect + parameter combo ("Buzz — Massive") and stamp it onto slices with a click.

Arrange pads. Reorganize the effect palette: click two pads to swap them. The Move's pad LEDs re-light as you go, and the layout is saved with presets. Factory layout is one click away.

Layouts. Save the entire pattern — seed, densities, resolution, pins — as a named layout. Applying one re-rolls the current loop into that exact pattern; the audio is untouched. Export/import as text to share.

Version note. The Tool tab and live sync need a schwung release newer than v0.11.4. On older releases the smack-in slot editor still works — reload the page to see pad-side changes.
09 · Dual mono

Two synths, one Smack

Running two mono synths into Move's stereo line-in? Flip Channels to Dual Mono (pad 74, or the Shift knob layer) and Smack treats left and right as two independent signals — each with its own effect pattern, own slice order, own pins — then places them in the stereo field with Pan L / Pan R (equal-power, LFO-targetable from Master FX).

Lane select (pad 75) picks which lane the steps and palette edit — cyan for left, orange-red for right. Hold it and turn knobs 1–2 to set the pans. Both lanes share one seed stream, so dual-mono patterns are just as reproducible as stereo ones, and the A side always plays the raw stereo input untouched.
